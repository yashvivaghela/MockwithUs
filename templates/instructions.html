Instructions
  1. Be professional: All participants are expected to dress and behave professionally, as they would in a traditional in-person interview.
  2. Test the technology: Participants are responsible for testing their internet connection and video conferencing software prior to the interview.
  3. Avoid interruptions: Participants should avoid interruptions, such as phone calls or notifications, during the interview.
  4. Be prepared: Participants should prepare for the interview as they would for a traditional in-person interview, by researching the company and the role, and having answers to common interview questions ready.
  5. Position the camera correctly: Participants should position the camera at eye level and make sure that their face and upper body are clearly visible.
  6. Check the lighting: Participants should make sure that their face is well-lit, without any harsh shadows or bright lights behind them.
  7. Keep still: Participants should avoid fidgeting, shifting in their seat, or making distracting movements during the interview.
